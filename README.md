# exploratory-Data-Analysis_student_performance_data_insight

🎯 Dataset Overview

This dataset explores how lifestyle and behavioral factors influence student productivity, burnout levels, focus index, and exam performance.



🔎 Key Features Included:

👤 Demographics:

      Age, Gender, Academic Level

📚 Study Habits:

      Study Hours, Self-Study Hours, Online Class Hours

📱 Digital Behavior:

      Social Media Usage, Gaming Hours, Total Screen Time

🏃 Health & Lifestyle:

      Sleep Hours, Exercise Minutes, Caffeine Intake, Mental Health Score

⚖ Work & Academic Factors:

       Part-Time Job, Upcoming Deadlines, Internet Quality, Focus Index, Burnout Level, Productivity Score, Exam Score

📊 Analytical Focus

To better understand the relationships within the data, I structured my analysis into the following groups:

📚 Study Habits:

 study_hours, self_study_hours, online_classes_hours

📱 Digital Behavior:

 social_media_hours, gaming_hours, screen_time_hours

🧠 Mental Health:

 mental_health_score, burnout_level, sleep_hours

🎓 Academic Performance:

 exam_score, productivity_score, focus_index
 

📊 Key Insights:

• Study intensity drives performance.

 Total study hours show a moderate positive correlation with exam score (0.51) and productivity (0.64), reinforcing effort as a key performance driver.

• Mental health is a critical performance factor.

 Mental health score demonstrates moderate positive relationships with exam score (0.55), productivity (0.63), and focus (0.58), while burnout negatively impacts exam outcomes (-0.41).

 This suggests psychological well-being is a significant predictor of academic success.

• Digital behavior primarily affects focus.

 Social media usage shows a noticeable negative correlation with focus (-0.31), with weaker negative relationships across performance metrics.

 The impact appears indirect, affecting concentration more than exam scores directly.

• Performance metrics are strongly interrelated.

 Exam score, productivity, and focus index show very strong correlations (0.75–0.89), indicating they reinforce one another within the academic performance system.


🎯 Conclusion

The data suggests academic outcomes are multidimensional, driven not only by study effort, but significantly influenced by mental health and behavioral patterns.

Understanding these interactions is key to building predictive models and designing effective student performance interventions.
